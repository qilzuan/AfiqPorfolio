Fabrication and Characterisation of Cu and Co loaded on Nickel Sheet Catalyst for Ammonia electro-oxidation

Post By Muhammad Afiq bin Abdullah Sedek
Category : Chemical Technology/Catalyst

In my FYP project, I developed a catalyst by electrodepositing copper and cobalt salts onto a nickel sheet to enhance ammonia electro-oxidation for hydrogen production. Characterization involved using UV-Vis spectroscopy to measure ammonia concentration, X-ray diffraction (XRD) to analyze crystal structure, and Field Emission Scanning Electron Microscopy (FESEM) to assess surface morphology and catalytic activity. I further optimized reaction conditions through Response Surface Methodology (RSM) with a Box-Behnken Design, refining parameters to maximize catalyst efficiency in hydrogen generation. This project honed my skills in catalyst fabrication, electrochemical processes, and advanced material characterization techniques.
